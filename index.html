United States Digital Asset Regulations

Under the Internal Revenue Code (IRC) § 6045 and final broker reporting rules (2026), all digital asset transactions must be reported. The IRS treats cryptocurrency as property, subject to capital gains tax. Key compliance mandates:

  • Form 1099-DA: Brokers (including certain DeFi platforms) must report gross proceeds and cost basis for each crypto sale starting tax year 2025.
  • Cost Basis Tracking: Specific identification or FIFO required. Failure to track may result in default FIFO by IRS.
  • Sanctions & OFAC: US persons cannot transact with sanctioned addresses. Any violation leads to account suspension and severe penalties (up to $20M per violation).
  • Penalties for Non-Reporting: Underreporting crypto gains triggers accuracy-related penalties (20-40% of underpayment). Criminal evasion can lead to 5 years imprisonment.

Anti-Money Laundering (AML): The Bank Secrecy Act requires cryptocurrency exchanges to report transactions > $10,000. FinCEN enforces strict KYC/AML rules. Users must never share private keys; the IRS may request wallet addresses via John Doe summons.

Cross-border compliance: Citizens residing in OFAC-sanctioned countries are prohibited from using US-regulated crypto services. If identified, accounts will be frozen and reported to OFAC.

Official IRS crypto guidance: irs.gov/crypto · For questions, consult a licensed tax attorney.
